Section 1.0: Graph Theory

Simple Graphs

Def: A simple graph G consists of a non-empty set V, called the vertices (aka nodes) of G and a set E of two-element subset of V. The memebers of E are called the edges of G. G can be denoted as G = (V,E).

Def: Two vertices in a simple graph are said to be adjacent if they are joined by an edge. The edge is said to be incident to the vertices it joins. The number of edges incident to a vertex v is called the degree of the vertex.

Some Common Graphs

  • The complete graph on n vertices, Kn, has an edge between every two vertices for a total of n(n-1)/2 edges.
  • The empty graph has no edges
  • The n-node graph containing n - 1 edges in sequence is known as the line graph, Ln. Ln = (V,E) where

V = {v1, v2, ..., vn}

E = {{v1, v2}, {v2, v3}, ..., {vn-1, vn}}

Isomorphism

Def: If G1 = (V1, E1 and G2 = (V2, E2) are two graphs, then we say G1 is isomorphic to G2 iff there exists a bijection f:V1->V2 such that for ever pair of vertices u,v \exists V1

{u,v} \exists E1 iff {f(u), f(v)} \exists E2

The function f is called an isomorphism between G1 and G2.
